# Regulations — Paired Statutory vs Contractual Index

The two source registers in this folder present a **statutory-tenure (Norway) vs.
contractual / concession-embedded (UAE)** contrast for CCS/CCUS governance.
In Norway the four governance gaps are resolved by dedicated statute and regulation; in the
UAE they are left to petroleum concessions and the JOAs beneath them.

| Governance gap | Norway resolution | UAE resolution |
|----------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Storage tenure** | Statute/regulation — dedicated exploitation licence under CO₂ Storage Regulations 2014 (Continental Shelf Act s. 3); separate from the petroleum estate | JOA/contract — pore space flows from emirate petroleum concession title; no severable storage estate |
| **Post-closure / long-term liability transfer** | Statute — Ch 7 CO₂ Storage Regulations provides statutory pathway for stewardship transfer to the Norwegian State | JOA/contract — no codified transfer mechanism; residual obligation sits inside the JOA or reverts to ADNOC as concession holder |
| **Pollution liability** | Statute — Ch 8 CO₂ Storage Regulations; supplemented by Pollution Control Act 1981 | JOA/contract — no CCS-specific pollution statute; standard concession/JOA liability clauses |
| **MRV** | Statute/regulation — monitoring plan mandated as licence condition; data submitted to Norwegian Offshore Directorate (Sodir); eventually published | JOA/contract + federal Climate Law — UAE Climate Law 2024 (in force May 2025) creates federal MRV obligation; emirate MRV via EAD; but monitoring detail governed by concession/JOA, not dedicated storage regulation |
