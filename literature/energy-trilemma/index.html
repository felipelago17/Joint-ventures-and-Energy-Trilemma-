    Literature: Energy Trilemma¶

    Annotated reading list on the World Energy Council energy trilemma framework — security, equity, and environmental sustainability.

    Foundational Texts¶

    Bradshaw, M.J. (2014) Global Energy Dilemmas: Energy Security, Globalization, and Climate Change. Polity Press.

    Examines the three overarching tensions in the global energy system — energy security, economic development, and climate responsibility — using a political-economy lens. Provides comparative state-level context for assessing how Norway (a managed producer-state) and UAE (a Gulf petrostate) face structurally different versions of the trilemma, with direct implications for how their JV frameworks are designed to manage these tensions.

    Horúcková, M. & Baudassé, T. (2019) Is there a trilemma of energy policy? A theoretical and empirical approach, Economic Research Guardian, 9(1), 2–34.

    Constructs three composite energy-policy indexes (security, sustainability, environmental protection) using PCA for eight European countries and tests whether the trilemma dimensions are inherently incompatible. Provides theoretical grounding for the claim that trilemma trade-offs are real, not rhetorical — a prerequisite for applying the framework to JV governance analysis.

    Khan, I., Zakari, A., Dagar, V. & Singh, S. (2022) World energy trilemma and transformative energy developments as determinants of economic growth amid environmental sustainability, Energy Economics, 108, 105884.

    Uses panel data across major economies to show that trilemma balance simultaneously supports economic growth and environmental sustainability, but that investment in non-financial assets and fossil fuel use creates a trade-off between growth and sustainability. Relevant for framing how petrostates such as Norway and UAE navigate the growth-versus-decarbonisation dilemma within JV investment decisions.

    Song, L., Fu, Y., Zhou, P. & Lai, K.K. (2017) Measuring national energy performance via Energy Trilemma Index: A stochastic multicriteria acceptability analysis, Energy Economics, 66, 313–319.

    Applies stochastic multicriteria acceptability analysis to validate and stress-test the WEC Trilemma Index's composite scoring methodology for 125 countries. Directly relevant for operationalising the trilemma as a measurable construct in comparative Norway/UAE analysis, and for critiquing whether single-index country rankings adequately capture hydrocarbon-dependent economies.

    Sovacool, B.K. & Brown, M.A. (2010) Competing dimensions of energy security: An international perspective, Annual Review of Environment and Resources, 35, 77–108.

    Surveys academic literature on energy security and identifies four core, often competing, dimensions: availability, affordability, efficiency, and environmental stewardship. Establishes the intellectual lineage of multi-dimensional energy security frameworks and explains why hydrocarbon-dependent states like Norway and UAE rank differently on different trilemma pillars.

    Sovacool, B.K. & Mukherjee, I. (2011) Conceptualizing and measuring energy security: A synthesized approach, Energy, 36(8), 5343–5355.

    Synthesises 320 simple and 52 complex indicators across five dimensions of energy security (availability, affordability, technology, sustainability, regulation) into a unified framework. Essential methodological reference for disaggregating the "security" pillar of the WEC trilemma when assessing how JV governance arrangements in Norway and UAE contribute to or detract from security of supply.
